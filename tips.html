üåç Earthquake

      Before:

    • Secure heavy furniture to walls.
    • Keep a family emergency plan and go-bag ready.
    • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On."

      During:

    • Indoors:Get under sturdy furniture, protect your head.
    • Outdoors: Move away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
    • In a vehicle: Pull over safely and stay inside.

      After:

    • Expect aftershocks.
    • Check for gas leaks and electrical damage.
    • Listen to emergency broadcasts and stay calm.

üåä Flood

      Before:

    • Know your flood zone and evacuation routes.
    • Keep important documents in waterproof containers.
    • Elevate appliances and switch off electricity at the main breaker.

      During:

    • Evacuate early if instructed.
    • Never walk or drive through floodwater.
    • Move to higher ground immediately.

      After:

    • Avoid waterlogged areas ‚Äî risk of electrocution and contamination.
    • Disinfect everything that got wet.
    • Boil drinking water until declared safe.

üåÄ Cyclone

      Before:

    • Board up windows, bring loose items indoors.
    • Know shelter locations and prepare emergency kits.
    • Charge power banks and fill clean water containers.

      During:

    • Stay indoors and away from windows.
    • Avoid using candles (fire risk); use flashlights instead.
    • Monitor news and alerts closely.

      After:

    • Watch out for fallen power lines and flooded roads.
    • Don't drink tap water unless declared safe.
    • Contact family and let them know you're safe.

üë£ Stampede

      Before:

    • Avoid overcrowded areas when possible.
    • Know where exits are when you enter.
    • Keep hydrated and wear light clothing and secure footwear.

      During:

    • Stay on your feet. Fall = High risk.
    • Don't push or resist the flow ‚Äì move diagonally to safety.
    • Protect your chest with arms like a boxer.
    • If you fall, curl into a ball, cover your head and try to get up quickly.

      After:

    • Help others if you are safe.
    • Move to open areas quickly.
    • Seek first aid for bruises, crush injuries, or panic attacks.

üî• Fire / Wildfire

      Before:

    • Clear dry vegetation around your home.
    • Install smoke alarms and fire extinguishers.
    • Prepare a "go-bag" with essentials.

      During:

    • Cover mouth and nose with a cloth (to avoid smoke).
    • Follow evacuation orders immediately.
    • Crawl low under smoke indoors.

      After:

    • Do not return until officials say it's safe.
    • Watch for flare-ups or smoldering debris.
    • Get medical help if breathing difficulty occurs.

‚õà Thunderstorm

      Before:

    • Avoid open fields and metal objects.
    • Unplug electronics.

      During:

    • Stay indoors and away from windows.
    • Avoid water usage (don't bathe or wash).
    • If outside, squat low with minimal ground contact.

      After:

    • Watch for downed power lines.
    • Stay away from waterlogged areas.

ü¶† Pandemic/Disease Outbreak

      Before:

    • Maintain hygiene habits: handwashing, mask use.
    • Store necessary medical supplies.

      During:

    • Follow public health guidance.
    • Avoid crowded places.
    • Stay isolated if sick.

      After:

    • Continue hygiene and vaccinations.
    • Be mentally supportive of others in recovery.
    • Stay informed about new outbreaks.

‚òÄ Heatwave

      Before:

    • Stay hydrated and wear light clothing.
    • Know locations of cooling shelters.

      During:

    • Avoid outdoor activities during peak heat.
    • Drink water often ‚Äî even if not thirsty.
    • Use wet cloths and fans to cool down.

      After:

    • Monitor for heat stroke symptoms (dizziness, confusion).
    • Continue staying cool as temperatures may remain high.

‚ùÑ Extreme Cold

      Before:

    • Stock up on food, water, blankets, and batteries.
    • Insulate home and pipes.

      During:

    • Stay indoors; if outside, wear layered clothing.
    • Avoid overexertion ‚Äî can cause heart strain.
    • If in a car, stay inside and run heater periodically.

      After:

    • Check on neighbors and elderly.
    • Beware of ice on roads and sidewalks.
    • Don't use gas stoves for heating ‚Äî risk of CO poisoning.

ü™® Landslide

      Before:

    • Avoid building on slopes.
    • Watch for cracks in ground or leaning trees.
    • Keep emergency kit ready and stay updated on weather alerts.

      During:

    • Move away from the path of the slide.
    • Go to higher ground quickly.
    • Avoid roads near hills or flowing debris.

      After:

    • Stay away from the area until safe.
    • Report damaged structures or utility lines.
    • Help others if safe and call emergency services.
